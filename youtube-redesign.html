YouTube's mobile viewing experience reimagined: DSGN 1
Goal: To overcome obtrusive and nonintuitive features within YouTube's landscape mode.


Collaborators: Winson Dieu, Evon Hung, Lauren Choy, Madeline Ng, Kyoko Ishikawa.

Intro: Choosing an existing product to redesign was no easy feat for our team. Although there existed an infinite amount of ideas to fixate on, we selected YouTube. It is constantly being revamped, rolling out new features each update. This set high expectations for the user interface. After examining the user interface on different devices, we created a mind map that helped us select YouTube's mobile app as our chosen product.


Our mindmap zoned in on three topics: Devices, User Interface, and Features.

So why the mobile version instead?: The mobile version contains a greater amount of design constraints due to the limited physical space that exists on a smartphone or tablet. These constraints often lead to compromises within the user's experience and the creation of discrepancies between the website and app versions.

The Data Collection Process: Prior to stepping into user research, our team conducted a breakdown of the app in order to seek out pain points. Some of us had experience within the app prior to the project, while others did not. This allowed us to better extract possible flaws, as the people with less exposure to the app are able to pinpoint them more efficiently. Drawing from the slips and mistakes our team made while using the app, we devised a set of instructions for our users to follow during testing.
